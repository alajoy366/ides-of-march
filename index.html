For over two thousand years, the Ides of March has belonged to men who were impossible to ignore. Caesar built an empire and was betrayed by the men he trusted most. Pargalı Ibrahim stood at the right hand of the greatest sultan in Ottoman history — loyal, brilliant, irreplaceable — and was strangled in his sleep by the same man he gave everything to. Both warriors. Both betrayed. Both remembered not for how they fell, but for the scale of what they were. You call them your ancestors. Looking at this archive, it is not hard to see why. The loyalty, the certainty, the refusal to be anything less than fully yourself — it is the same thread, pulled across twenty-one centuries and landing, on March 15th 2005, in Bangladesh. This date has always known what it was doing. It just took two thousand years to arrive at you.

Happy 21st birthday, Ahmed.  —  Alajoy ♡
